The business problem

Rooms often work on day one. The cost appears when ownership drifts.

Without an operating model, AV failures are discovered by users, escalated through ticket noise, and translated into wasted IT time, poor executive experience, and surprise refresh events.

Reactive AV

Vendor response after the room fails.

  • Failures surface when someone walks into the room.
  • Tickets start the process after confidence is already damaged.
  • Reporting stays anecdotal; refresh needs arrive as budget surprises.

Managed by IMS

Operator accountability before the meeting begins.

  • Rooms are monitored as an environment, not treated as isolated installs.
  • Issues are detected earlier and resolved through support accountability.
  • Reporting, recommendations, and lifecycle planning stay connected.

The IMS shift

IMS does not just install AV. IMS operates it.

The process is intentionally sequential because each action changes the quality of the next executive decision.

  1. Monitor

    Keep watch on room health and service readiness.

  2. Detect

    Identify issues before they become executive friction.

  3. Resolve

    Move support from ad hoc response to accountable action.

  4. Report

    Create visibility for IT, operations, and leadership.

  5. Recommend

    Turn usage and condition into planning guidance.

  6. Refresh

    Make renewals and upgrades planned, not panicked.
